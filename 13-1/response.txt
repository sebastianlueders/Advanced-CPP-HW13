Feature test macros are defined as macros because feature detection often needs to happen during preprocessing, before the compiler parses C++ code. This enables
portable #if based conditional compilation, including selecting headers/declarations and avoiding unsupported syntax. constexpr variables are compile time C++ 
entities, but they are not visible to the preprocessor, so they cannot serve this role. This preprocessor-level portability and control is probably why WG21
standardized feature-test macros.
